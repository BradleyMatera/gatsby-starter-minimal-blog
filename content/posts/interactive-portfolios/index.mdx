Interactive portfolios are fun. They can also confuse people.

This post shows how I keep mine readable for recruiters and still fun to browse.

You will get the rules I follow, a checklist, and the mistakes to avoid.

## The promise

You will learn:

- when to use motion
- how to keep the content scannable
- how to avoid misleading claims

> "Fun is fine. Clarity is required."

## The rules I follow

- Start with a clear headline.
- Keep navigation obvious.
- Write short summaries with proof links.

### Example content block

```md
Problem: short and direct
Solution: short and direct
Proof: link to repo or demo
```

## Now do it your way

### Steps

1. Write a one sentence summary for each project.
2. Add one proof link.
3. Limit motion to one or two sections.

### Common mistakes

- Hiding the main content behind animations.
- Using vague claims with no links.
- Overloading the hero with text.

## Quick checklist

- [ ] Clear nav
- [ ] Proof links
- [ ] Simple summaries
