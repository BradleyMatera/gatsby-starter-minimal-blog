## TL;DR

- **Learning is a permanent sprint.** I mix certifications, free courses, and experiments so knowledge compounds instead of expiring.  
- **Roadmaps come from exam guides.** AWS blueprints become checklists that steer weekly study and reveal gaps fast [^exam-guide].  
- **Practice beats memorisation.** Labs, flashcards, and teach-backs force me to apply concepts before exam day.  
- **Credentials feed real work.** Each badge pushes improvements into architecture reviews, interview drills, and documentation habits.

- **AWS Solutions Architect – Associate (Aug 2024 → Aug 2027):** Architect resilient, cost-aware AWS workloads; schedule renewal six months early [^saa].  
- **AWS Certified AI Practitioner (Feb 2025 → Feb 2028):** Grounded responsible AI usage and Bedrock deployment patterns [^ai-practitioner].

I log expirations in Notion, add reminders six months ahead, and pair each renewal with a project to prove retention.

## Study System

### Start with the guide

- Download the official exam outline, convert domains to a status spreadsheet, and revisit progress weekly [^exam-guide].

### Blend theory with reps

- **Hands-on labs:** Run AWS Skill Builder scenarios and spin up disposable environments to build intuition.  
- **Spaced repetition:** Maintain Anki decks for acronyms, service limits, and scenario cues.  
- **Teach-backs:** Record Loom explainers; if the story is fuzzy, loop the topic back into study sessions.

### Reflect immediately

- Post-exam, capture weak topics, schedule mini-projects (e.g., Bedrock-powered accessibility copy) to convert notes into working code.

## Ship What You Learn

- **Side projects:** Apply well-architected reviews, multi-AZ defaults, and IaC templates before marking anything "done."  
- **Interview prep:** Reuse algorithm drills to narrate data-structure choices quickly.  
- **Documentation:** Soft-skill courses influence retrospectives, stakeholder email templates, and portfolio narratives.

## Keep Momentum Alive

1. **Quarterly themes:** Choose a focus (security automation, data engineering) to align books, courses, and experiments.  
2. **Accountability:** Share progress with a study circle and LinkedIn posts so goals stay visible.  
3. **Depth + breadth:** Earn badges for fundamentals while exploring emerging tech like Zig and WebGPU for curiosity.

Continuous learning works when study, practice, and teaching form a tight loop. Every credential is a prompt to ship something better.

## Key Takeaways

- **Map the exam before you open the book.** A checklist keeps studying intentional.  
- **Reps create recall.** Labs, flashcards, and teach-backs embed concepts deeper than rereading.  
- **Translate credentials into impact.** Document how each course or exam changes your architecture, communication, or interview game.
